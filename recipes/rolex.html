Rolex

Description

A Ugandan Rolex isn't a luxury watch; it's Uganda's beloved street food: a chapati (flatbread) wrapped around a fresh egg omelet with mixed vegetables like tomatoes, onions, and cabbage, forming a savory, handheld meal known for its name "rolled eggs".

Found on every street corner, this affordable, quick, and filling dish is a cultural staple enjoyed by all, from students to workers, for breakfast, lunch, or a snack, often with added fillings like meat.

Ingredients

  • Chapati: A soft, unleavened flatbread. While store-bought versions work, authentic street-style chapatis are made from wheat flour, water, salt, and oil.
  • Eggs: Typically 2 per Rolex, though more can be used for a heartier meal.
  • Onions: Finely chopped or sliced rings (usually red onions).
  • Tomatoes: Thinly sliced or diced for juiciness.
  • Cabbage: Thinly shredded and added either into the egg mix or as a fresh garnish before rolling.
  • Salt: Used to season the eggs and vegetables.
  • Vegetable Oil: Essential for frying the omelet and the chapati.
  • Vegetables: Bell peppers (hoho), grated carrots, or fresh coriander (cilantro).
  • Proteins: Shredded chicken, minced beef, or sausages.
  • Extras: Sliced avocado, cheese, or minced garlic.
  • Seasoning: Black pepper or small green chilies for a spicy kick.

Steps

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Thinly slice the onion and tomatoes, and shred the cabbage. If using optional ingredients like chilies or cilantro, chop them finely.
  2. Whisk the eggs: In a bowl, crack the eggs and whisk them with a pinch of salt and pepper. Stir in all the prepared vegetables.
  3. Warm the chapati: Heat a non-stick pan or skillet over medium heat. Warm the chapati on both sides for about a minute in total to make it soft and pliable. Set it aside and cover to keep warm.
  4. Cook the omelet: In the same pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Pour in the egg mixture and swirl the pan to distribute it evenly over the surface.
  5. Combine the chapati and egg: Once the bottom of the egg mixture is set, place the warmed chapati directly on top of the omelet. Press down gently so they stick together.
  6. Flip and cook: Carefully flip the chapati and egg over so the chapati side is now on the hot surface of the pan. Cook for another 1-2 minutes until the chapati is lightly browned and the egg is fully cooked.
  7. Roll and serve: Transfer the chapati and omelet onto a clean plate. Starting from one edge, roll it up tightly into a log shape to form the "Rolex".
  8. Enjoy: Slice the Rolex in half or serve it whole, immediately while still warm. It can be wrapped in foil or paper for an authentic street-food experience.
